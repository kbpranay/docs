<Callout type="info">
  **Channels vs. Integrations** — think of channels as the *surface* where users meet your bot (a web widget, a phone call, WhatsApp). For connecting your bot to external systems like CRMs, databases, or custom APIs, see **[Integrations (Flows)](/integrations)**.
</Callout>

## Managing a channel

Every channel has an enable/disable toggle on the Go-Live page. Only **App Admin**, **Team Owner**, **Manager**, and **AIDA Admin** can change the status.

| Channel | Behavior when disabled |
|---|---|
| **Web/App (Chatbot)** | The chat widget stops loading for new sessions. Ongoing conversations continue until the page is refreshed. |
| **WhatsApp** | Ongoing sessions continue and users can still send agent requests until session auto-closes. New messages receive an offline message. |
| **Voice** | Ongoing calls proceed as normal. New incoming calls are disconnected and receive an offline message. |

<Callout>
  The offline message for Voice and WhatsApp is **editable**. Click outside the field after editing to auto-save.

  Interactions that happen while a channel is disabled are not included in training data — they're only counted under **Total Conversations** in Live Data and Analytics.
</Callout>

## Bot readiness

Your bot's status on the Home Page tells you what's left before it can go live:

| State | Status shown |
|---|---|
| Created from scratch / pre-built intents | In Progress |
| DocBrain indexing failed | Indexing Failed |
| All mandatory criteria met | Your bot is now ready to go live |

## Accessing Go-Live

Click **Go-Live** from the side menu, or hit the **Go-Live Recommended** button on the Home Page.
