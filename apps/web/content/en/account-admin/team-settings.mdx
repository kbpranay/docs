# Team Settings

Team Settings holds everything that applies to your whole team — the team's display name, two-factor authentication (2FA), and the lockout policy for failed logins.

Open it from **Team Settings** in the navigation.

## Updating the team name

<Steps>

### Open Team Settings → Details

Click the **Team settings** tab, then open the **Details** section.

### Edit the team name

Click on the team name field and change the value.

### Save

Click **[Save]**. *(Use **Reset** to discard unsaved changes.)*

</Steps>

<Callout>
  The **Organization name** and **Team ID** are set at team creation and cannot be changed afterwards. Only the team name is editable.
</Callout>

## Two-factor authentication (2FA)

2FA adds a second verification step at login — on top of a password, users must also enter a code generated by an Authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password, etc.).

### Enabling 2FA for the team

<Steps>

### Open Team Settings → Security

Click **Team settings**, then **Security**.

### Toggle 2FA on

Turn on the **2FA** toggle.

### Save

Click **[Save]**.

</Steps>

Once enabled, every team member going through login sees a **[Setup]** button instead of **[Sign In]** until they complete 2FA setup.

### Setting up 2FA (end user flow)

<Steps>

### Enter email & password

Enter credentials as usual.

### Click Setup

Click **[Setup]** when prompted.

### Enter the code from email

An authentication code is emailed to the user. Enter it and click **[Validate]**.

### Scan the QR code

Scan the displayed QR code with an Authenticator app.

### Go to Sign In

Click **[Go to Sign in]**. Future logins will require a time-based code from the Authenticator app.

</Steps>

### Resetting another user's 2FA

If a user loses access to their Authenticator (new phone, lost device), an admin can reset their 2FA so they can set it up again.

| Your role | You can reset 2FA for |
|---|---|
| **Team Owner** | All users — including other Team Owners, Managers, and yourself |
| **Manager** | Users below Manager level: Admin, User, Dashboard, API & Services, Migration, LLM Labs |

<Callout>
  If you disable 2FA team-wide, users who had already set up 2FA still need their 2FA **individually reset** by a Team Owner or Manager before they can log in without it.
</Callout>

## Account lockout policy

To protect against brute-force login attempts, AIDA locks accounts after too many failed attempts. The defaults apply across all teams:

| Setting | Default |
|---|---|
| **Invalid attempts before lockout** | 5 |
| **Lockout duration** | 30 minutes |
| **Minimum time between attempts** | 60 seconds |

The Team Owner and Manager can adjust the max attempts and lockout duration from **Team Settings → Security**.
